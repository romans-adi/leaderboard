Chess Rules

Chess is a strategic board game played on an 8x8 grid. Players aim to checkmate the opponent's king. Each player starts with 16 pieces: king, queen, rooks, knights, bishops, and pawns. Pieces have specific movement rules: king moves 1 square in any direction, queen moves in any direction, rooks move horizontally or vertically, bishops move diagonally, knights move in an "L" shape, and pawns move forward 1 square or 2 squares on the first move, capturing diagonally. Special moves include castling (king and rook move together) and en passant (pawn capturing diagonally). The game ends when a king is checkmated, in stalemate, or if players agree to a draw.
